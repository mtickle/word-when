Navigating big feelings with kids can be tough. That is why I built “WordWhen”, an app designed to help children (or even adults) map their complex emotions to specific, comforting Bible verses. Specifically, I wrote this for Piper when she was going through a season of struggles. It was very uplifting to hear her say, “I need to go to WordWhen for a minute.” Instead of generic advice, the app offers targeted scripture to help users find peace when things feel overwhelming. It is a simple tool to help children process their day and ground their hearts. I was able to use it as a springboard for bigger discussions about emotions, showing how the teachings in the Bible can help calm the mind and bring things into perspective.

The framework for understanding our emotions has hundreds of years of research behind it. Starting with René Descartes in 1649 and evolving into Robert Plutchik's Wheel of Emotions in 1980, science has long tried to map out exactly how humans feel. Word When combines this proven psychological framework with the timeless truth of scripture. By categorizing 48 distinct feelings into 8 core emotions, the app pairs psychological clarity with the perfect Bible verse to bring comfort and perspective.

I wanted Word When to be accessible exactly when it is needed most. That is why I built it as a Progressive Web App. You do not need to hunt through an app store or wait for a heavy download. You can install it directly to your phone's home screen for instant access. Whether you are at home or on the go, comfort and clarity are just one tap away.

There are no ads, and no tracking in this app. Any updates will happen automatically. Please let me know directly if you have any problems!

How to Install on iOS (iPhone & iPad)

To install the app on an Apple device, you will need to use the default Safari browser.
Open Safari and navigate to wordwhen.app.
Tap the Share button at the bottom of the screen. It looks like a square with an arrow pointing up.
Scroll down the list of options and tap Add to Home Screen.
You will see a preview of the app icon and name. Tap Add in the top right corner.
Word When will now appear on your home screen, ready to use just like a standard app.

How to Install on Android

To install the app on an Android device, using Google Chrome is the most seamless method.
Open Chrome and navigate to wordwhen.app.
Tap the Menu icon in the top right corner. It looks like three vertical dots.
Scroll down the menu and tap Install app or Add to Home screen.
A confirmation prompt will appear. Tap Install or Add to confirm.
Word When is now installed and can be found on your home screen or in your app drawer.
